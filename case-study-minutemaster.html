Automating Meeting Minutes

How AutomataX developed MinuteMaster to streamline the creation, formatting, and distribution of meeting minutes, saving time and ensuring consistency.

Client Overview

A mid-sized organization conducting frequent meetings across multiple departments relied on manual processes to document meeting minutes. Staff spent hours typing, formatting, and distributing minutes, leading to delays, inconsistent formats, and administrative overhead.

Challenges

  • Manual documentation: Recording meeting details, attendees, and action items was time-consuming and error-prone.
  • Inconsistent formatting: Minutes varied in style and structure, reducing professionalism and readability.
  • Distribution delays: Manually emailing minutes to attendees was repetitive and inefficient.
  • Template management: Maintaining consistent templates across teams was challenging without centralized control.
  • Lack of automation: No system existed to streamline the end-to-end process of minute creation and sharing.

Our Solution

We developed MinuteMaster, a Python-based desktop application with a PySide6 GUI, to automate the creation and management of meeting minutes:

  • Intuitive GUI: Provided a user-friendly interface for inputting meeting details, attendees, and discussion points.
  • PDF generation: Used ReportLab to create professional, consistently formatted PDF minutes with customizable templates.
  • Email automation: Integrated SMTP to send minutes to attendees automatically, with configurable email templates.
  • Configuration management: Allowed users to customize templates, SMTP settings, and branding via a settings dialog.
  • Real-time logging: Included a log viewer to track actions and errors, enhancing transparency and debugging.

Implementation Process

Phase 1: Requirements & Design

Gathered client requirements, analyzed existing minute formats, and designed a GUI with input forms and configuration options.

Phase 2: Development

Built the application using PySide6 for the GUI, ReportLab for PDF generation, and smtplib for email automation, with robust error handling and logging.

Phase 3: Testing & Deployment

Tested with sample meeting data, refined PDF layouts and email functionality, deployed the application, and provided user training for adoption.
